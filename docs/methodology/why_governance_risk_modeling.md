# Why Governance Risk Modeling

Governance risk modeling provides a reproducible way to combine competition, concentration, procedure, category, and network signals. Logistic regression supports interpretability, random forests capture nonlinear interactions, and gradient boosting-style models provide a stronger predictive benchmark.

The label is a governance risk proxy, not a legal finding. The model is therefore evaluated as an intelligence layer: it prioritizes cases for review and supports dissertation analysis of risk structures.

Leakage controls are central because several raw fields help define the label. Those label-defining inputs are excluded from the model predictor set.
